    Study Plans & Tracker¶

    Three plans for three runways, plus a progress tracker whose checkboxes persist in your browser (localStorage — tick them as you go; they'll be waiting when you return). Whichever plan you run: read actively, drill after each section, and rehearse out loud — the regimen is what converts reading into interview performance.

    The 48-hour cram¶

    For the interview that's already scheduled. Ruthless triage — depth where it pays, callouts elsewhere.

    • Evening 1: The DevOps lens → Thinking in systems → Estimation (memorize the numbers table)
    • Evening 1: The framework — then announce it to an empty room twice
    • Day 2 morning: URL shortener + news feed, studied then rehearsed out loud, 40 min each
    • Day 2 afternoon: Cache failure modes, resilience patterns, delivery semantics — the three highest-yield follow-up pages
    • Day 2 evening: Drills — Foundations + case-study sections; re-drill misses
    • Morning of: Cheat sheets only. No new material. You know things.

    The 3-week interview sprint¶

    The standard prep arc — one pass of everything, weighted toward performance.

    • Week 1 — foundations & data: all of Foundations (2 pages/day), then Data & Storage cram-priority order: replication, partitioning, storage engines, rest as reading
    • Week 1: Framework + requirements & estimation; rehearse the 90-second opening against 3 prompts
    • Week 2 — components & first blood: Caching, Messaging, Distributed Systems sections; Networking cram notes
    • Week 2: four case studies studied-and-rehearsed (shortener, rate limiter, feed, chat) + first mock
    • Week 3 — altitude & polish: Communication & levels (record yourself once), one DevOps special rehearsed (metrics or CI/CD), 2 transfer prompts cold (question bank)
    • Week 3: Observability SLO + alerting pages, deployments, multi-region — the operability-close ammunition
    • Week 3: second mock + drills full pass; final days per the cram plan

    The 12-week mastery track¶

    The fall-in-love, Staff-trajectory path: everything, in order, with practice woven in. One section ≈ one week; the pace is deliberate — retention beats coverage.

    • Weeks 1–2: Foundations complete + framework; start the habit: one drill section per week, one estimation rep per day
    • Weeks 3–4: Networking + Data & Storage complete; rehearse shortener + KV store
    • Week 5: Caching + Messaging; rehearse rate limiter + notifications
    • Week 6: Distributed Systems complete — the fencing-token and metastability stories told out loud until fluent
    • Weeks 7–8: DevOps & Platform complete (your crown jewel — read every Staff+ callout twice); rehearse both CI/CD and scheduler specials
    • Week 9: Observability & SRE + Security; rehearse metrics system + log pipeline
    • Week 10: remaining case studies (feed, chat, video, proximity, payments); first mock
    • Week 11: Communication & levels deep work — record two sessions, run the Principal-layer-only exercise on three prompts; transfer prompts
    • Week 12: second + third mocks, drills full pass, weak-section re-reads; then one paper from resources per week, forever — that's the habit that compounds past the interview

    Progress tracker¶

    Every page in the Grail. Tick as you finish (read + drilled), not as you skim. State persists per-browser.
