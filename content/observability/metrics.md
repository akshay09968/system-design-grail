# Metrics & Prometheus

Prometheus won the metrics war so thoroughly that its design choices — pull-based scraping, labels, PromQL, client-side histograms — *are* the mental model of modern metrics. Each choice encodes a distributed-systems lesson worth knowing on its own merits, and two of them (the pull model, the histogram) carry fine print that decides whether your dashboards tell the truth.

## The model: scrape, don't accept

Prometheus **pulls**: services expose `/metrics` (current values, plain text); the server scrapes on its schedule (15–60 s), storing timestamped series identified by **name + label set** (`http_requests_total{service="api", status="500"}`).

Why pull, when push feels natural? It's [the backpressure argument](../foundations/thinking-in-systems.md) in monitoring costume: the *monitoring system* controls its own ingest rate — a misbehaving service can't flood it ([push-gateway-shaped systems get overrun by their noisiest client](../messaging/async-fundamentals.md); scrapers can't be). Pull also gives **up-ness for free** (a failed scrape *is* the availability signal — `up == 0` beats any heartbeat you'd remember to build) and makes target discovery explicit ([service discovery](../devops/kubernetes-workloads.md) feeds the scrape config; what's monitored is *declared*, auditable, versioned). The honest exceptions: short-lived jobs (pushgateway, grudgingly) and [egress-restricted/edge sources](../devops/cloud-networking.md) (remote-write). Counters + scraping also yield crash-resilient math: a counter only climbs; `rate()` computes per-second slopes across scrape gaps and restarts — which is why *counters-and-rates* beat pre-computed averages every time.

## RED and USE: the 90% answer

**RED** — for every *service*: **R**ate (requests/s), **E**rrors (failures/s), **D**uration (latency, *as a histogram*). **USE** — for every *resource*: **U**tilization (busy %), **S**aturation (queued work — [the leading indicator](../foundations/latency-throughput.md): run-queue depth, [pool waiters](../networking/fundamentals.md), [queue age](../messaging/async-fundamentals.md)), **E**rrors. RED tells you users are hurting; USE tells you which resource is why. Between them plus [the boundary metrics](fundamentals.md) (queues, pools, caches, breakers), you've covered what dashboards exist to show — and "RED for services, USE for resources" is a complete, defensible answer to "what would you monitor?"

## Histograms: the fine print your p99 depends on

Averages lie ([established](../foundations/latency-throughput.md)); percentiles require distributions; distributions at scale require **histograms** — counters per latency bucket (`≤10ms: 8k, ≤50ms: 19k, ≤250ms: 22k...`), cheap to record, aggregable across the fleet. The fine print that separates operators from dashboard-readers:

- **Percentiles are interpolated between bucket edges.** With buckets at 100 ms and 250 ms, a "p99 = 180 ms" is a *guess inside that bucket* — the real p99 could be 110 or 240. Bucket layout is an editorial decision about where you need precision; put edges near your [SLO thresholds](slos.md) or your SLO measurement is quantization noise. (Native/exponential histograms — auto-scaled buckets — are fixing this industry-wide; worth one sentence as the current direction.)
- **Never average percentiles.** The [fleet-wide "average p99" is meaningless](../foundations/latency-throughput.md); aggregate the *histograms* (sum the buckets), then take the percentile. This is precisely what histograms buy that pre-computed per-node percentiles (summaries) cannot: mergability.
- **Exemplars** — sample [trace IDs](tracing.md) attached to bucket counts: click the p99 spike, land on an actual slow trace. The [correlation thread](fundamentals.md), made concrete.

## PromQL in five patterns

Fluency is five idioms, not the whole language: `rate(counter[5m])` (per-second slope — the universal verb); `histogram_quantile(0.99, sum by (le) (rate(bucket[5m])))` (fleet p99, aggregated *correctly*); error ratio (`rate(errors)/rate(total)` — [ratios, not absolutes, for alerting](alerting.md)); saturation gauges compared to capacity; and `absent()`/`up` (the silence detectors). Everything else is composition.

## Scale and the cardinality bomb, revisited

One Prometheus holds millions of series comfortably; growth paths: **federation/hierarchy** (per-cluster Prometheus, global roll-up), **remote-write to horizontally-scalable backends** (Thanos/Mimir/Cortex-class: object-storage-backed, [the metrics case study's](../case-studies/metrics-system.md) whole subject), and **recording rules** (pre-compute expensive queries — [the read-path/write-path trade](../foundations/thinking-in-systems.md): spend write-time compute to make dashboard reads instant).

And the operational headline from [fundamentals](fundamentals.md), because it's a *metrics* incident above all: the **cardinality bomb** — one deploy adds `user_id` or `pod_name`-with-churn as a label; series count goes vertical; the TSDB's memory follows; the monitoring system dies first, taking the dashboards you'd use to debug it. Defenses: label linting in CI, per-scrape series limits, cardinality dashboards (top metrics by series count — watch the *derivative*), and the cultural rule: [identifiers go in traces and logs, never labels](fundamentals.md).

!!! ops "DevOps lens"
    The metrics platform's own runbook: **scrape health as a first-class dashboard** (`up`, scrape duration, samples dropped — a scrape timing out at 9.8 s of its 10 s interval is a metric silently missing its own deadline), **staleness and gaps** (restart storms leave holes; `rate()` forgives, humans misread — annotate deploys), **retention economics** (raw resolution for 15 days, downsampled roll-ups for a year — [the tiering instinct](../data/object-storage.md) applied to time series), and **the meta-alert** ([dead-man's switch](alerting.md): an always-firing alert whose *silence* means the alerting path is broken — the monitoring equivalent of a heartbeat). Incident genres: the cardinality bomb (above — the fix at 3 a.m. is dropping the offending label at ingest and paging the team that shipped it), the *scrape-interval beat frequency* (60 s scrapes of a 55 s spike = aliasing — sub-minute phenomena need sub-minute scrapes or [logs/traces instead](fundamentals.md)), and the *recording-rule drift* (dashboard shows the rule, rule's expression rotted after a label rename — test rules like code).

!!! staff "Staff+ altitude"
    Markers: (1) **Metrics taxonomy as governance** — naming conventions (`unit`-suffixed, namespace-prefixed), mandatory base labels (service/version/region), and bucket standards, shipped in the [paved-road SDK](fundamentals.md) so fleet-wide queries are *possible* ("sum of RED across 400 services" only works if 400 services agree what R is called). (2) **Own the platform economics** — series counts grow superlinearly with microservice count; the Thanos-vs-managed-vs-Datadog decision is a [build-vs-buy](../data/sql-at-scale.md) with cardinality as the price axis, re-run at scale milestones, with per-team showback for series the way [cost-capacity](../devops/cost-capacity.md) showbacks compute. (3) **Bucket standards near SLOs** — a platform-wide histogram layout with edges at the org's latency SLO thresholds turns "is p99 under 200 ms?" from interpolation into arithmetic; it's a one-hour decision that upgrades every SLO in the company. (4) **Aliasing literacy** — knowing what a 15 s scrape *cannot see* (sub-interval spikes, [thundering-herd microbursts](../caching/failure-modes.md)) and routing those questions to traces keeps the org from tuning dashboards to hallucinations.

!!! interview "In the interview"
    Set pieces: **why pull** (backpressure — the monitor controls its load; `up` for free; declared targets — three reasons, not "because Prometheus"); **RED/USE deployment** (services vs. resources, with saturation named as the leading indicator); and the **histogram depth-charge** — when latency comes up: *"histograms, aggregated then quantiled — never averaged percentiles — with bucket edges placed at the SLO boundary, and exemplars linking spikes to traces."* That sentence contains three mistakes-not-made; interviewers who've operated metrics systems hear all three. Probes: *"how do you get a fleet-wide p99?"* (sum the bucket rates by `le`, then `histogram_quantile` — and say *why* per-node percentiles can't merge); *"what breaks metrics systems?"* (cardinality, with the arithmetic and the CI-linting defense); *"push or pull and why?"* (pull, three reasons, then the honest exceptions: batch jobs, edge).
