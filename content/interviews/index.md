# Interview Playbook

Everything before this section made you *know* things. This section makes you *score* with them. A system design interview is a performance with a rubric, a clock, and an audience of one skeptic — and like [incident response](../observability/incidents.md), it rewards structure precisely because pressure destroys improvisation. The playbook: a framework you can run on any question, the technique pages that sharpen its two hardest phases, the communication layer that converts knowledge into signal, and the practice system that makes it automatic.

## If you are cramming

[The framework](framework.md) tonight, [cheat sheets](cheatsheets.md) the morning of, and one full [case study](../case-studies/index.md) rehearsed out loud — speaking, not reading. The out-loud part is non-negotiable: the interview is an oral exam, and reading silently trains the wrong muscle.
