# The Framework

Forty-five minutes, one open-ended prompt, and a rubric you can't see. The framework below is the standard seven-phase structure with the timing that fits, plus — more valuable — *what each phase is actually scoring* and the recovery plays for when it wobbles. Run it enough times ([practice page](question-bank.md)) and it stops being a checklist and becomes what it is for great candidates: the shape their thinking naturally takes. It's the same skill as [running an incident](../observability/incidents.md): structure that survives pressure.

## The seven phases (45-minute calibration)

**1. Requirements — 5 minutes.** Scope ruthlessly: functional ("which 3 features are core? I'll explicitly park the rest"), non-functional (scale, latency targets, [consistency needs per data class](../foundations/cap-pacelc.md), availability tier), and the constraints question that flavors everything ("read-heavy or write-heavy? global or regional?"). *Scoring: do you navigate ambiguity or charge into it?* The strongest single move: end the phase by *restating the agreed scope aloud* — it converts a fuzzy prompt into a contract, and interviewers remember who did that. ([Full technique page.](requirements-estimation.md))

**2. Estimation — 3 minutes.** [The recipes](../foundations/estimation.md): QPS, storage, bandwidth — ending with the **verdict**: "so the hard part is X." Estimation exists to *aim the interview*; a number without a consequence is theater. Skip the full ceremony when scale is given — but always do enough arithmetic to justify where you'll spend your time.

**3. API & data model — 5 minutes.** The core entities, their [key structure](../data/partitioning.md) (the shard key decision *starts here*), and the 3–5 endpoints/operations with [idempotency noted where it matters](../networking/apis.md). This is [the gap phase for ops-background candidates](../foundations/devops-lens.md) — do not rush it; interviewers specifically check whether infrastructure people can model data.

**4. High-level design — 10 minutes.** The [skeleton](../foundations/thinking-in-systems.md): stores, flows, transformations — boxes with *jobs*, arrows with *protocols*, and the [sync/async boundary drawn explicitly](../foundations/thinking-in-systems.md). Narrate as you draw; end with a full request traced through ("a write enters here, lands there, fans out to..."). *Scoring: coherence — does the shape serve the requirements from phase 1?* Resist detail — depth is the next phase's job, and diving early is the classic time-trap.

**5. Deep dives — 15 minutes. The interview is won here.** Two or three components, chosen by *where the hard part lives* (your phase-2 verdict) and steered by the interviewer's interest. This is where the whole site deploys: [the four questions](../foundations/thinking-in-systems.md) on your own design, [the bottleneck sequence](../foundations/scalability.md) ("at 10× this breaks first..."), the [named trade-offs with the template](../foundations/thinking-in-systems.md). Let the interviewer redirect you — their interruptions are the rubric talking.

**6. Operability — 4 minutes, your signature.** [The three moves](../foundations/devops-lens.md), compressed: *"Observability: [RED per service, queue age, trace propagation end-to-end](../observability/fundamentals.md). Rollout: [flag-gated, canary by cell, auto-rollback on burn](../devops/deployments.md). Failure story: [the two scariest boxes and their degraded modes](../distributed/resilience.md) — and the failover is [game-day'd](../observability/chaos.md)."* Most candidates don't have this phase at all; yours should be rehearsed to fluency.

**7. Wrap — 3 minutes.** Bottlenecks recap, what you'd build first vs. defer ([the staged honesty](../foundations/scalability.md)), and the trade-offs you'd revisit with more information. Ending with judgment — not apology — is the last impression that sticks.

## Recovery plays (the part nobody rehearses)

- **Blank on a component** → zoom out to first principles: "I haven't built this exact thing; reasoning from properties: it needs [ordered delivery per key, so something log-shaped](../messaging/kafka.md)..." Deriving beats remembering — and interviewers *prefer* watching derivation.
- **Caught in an error** → the [estimation-recovery move](../foundations/estimation.md) generalized: name it, fix it, move on. "Actually, that's wrong — at this write rate the cache-aside race matters; let me fix the invalidation." Self-correction is *positive* signal; defended errors are fatal.
- **Interviewer pushes back** → treat it as [a design review gift](../foundations/thinking-in-systems.md): steel-man their concern first ("you're right that the fan-out explodes at celebrity scale"), then either adapt or respectfully defend with a named trade-off. The worst response is agreeing without changing anything — that's neither conviction nor flexibility.
- **Time collapsing** → say the prioritization aloud: "ten minutes left — I'd rather deep-dive the [hot-partition story](../data/partitioning.md) than polish the API; sound right?" Managing the clock *visibly* is itself senior signal.
- **The mid-interview twist** ("now make it multi-region") → it's not sabotage, it's [the rubric's depth probe](communication.md); [ask why first](../devops/multi-region.md), then adapt the design *incrementally*, narrating what changes and what survives. Rigid designs that can't absorb twists score worse than rougher designs that bend.

!!! staff "Staff+ altitude"
    The framework is the same at every level; **the altitude of each phase changes** ([the full level map](communication.md)): requirements gain a *"should this exist / what's the simplest thing that works"* beat; estimation gains [cost verdicts](../devops/cost-capacity.md) alongside scale verdicts; high-level design gains explicit *evolution* ("v1 is a modular monolith with the partition key pre-planned; here's the seam we split at 10×" — [the staged-and-costed instinct](../foundations/scalability.md)); deep dives gain *organizational* trade-offs ([this boundary is a team boundary](../networking/apis.md)); and the wrap gains a risk register ("the bet I'm least sure of is X; here's how we'd find out cheaply"). At Principal altitude, expect fewer boxes and more *whether*: build-vs-buy, sequencing, what you'd refuse to build. The framework holds; the sentences inside it grow up.

!!! interview "In the interview"
    Meta-advice for the framework itself: **announce it** — thirty seconds up front ("I'll clarify scope, size it, sketch the data model and API, draw the high level, then spend most of our time deep in the hard parts — steer me anytime") buys enormous structural credit and permission to manage the clock; **timestamp your transitions** ("that's the high level — with 20 minutes left, let's go deep on the fan-out") so drift is impossible; and **let phase 5 breathe** — the framework's biggest failure mode is spending 30 minutes on phases 1–4 and leaving the actual scoring phase 8 minutes. The time split to internalize: *one-third setup, two-thirds depth.* And remember [the exception rule](communication.md): if the interviewer redirects, follow — the framework serves the conversation, never the reverse.
