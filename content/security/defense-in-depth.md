# Defense in Depth

Every security architecture is a bet about failure — and the only bet that survives contact with reality is: **any single control will eventually fail, so no single control may be load-bearing alone**. Defense in depth is [redundancy engineering](../foundations/reliability-availability.md) applied to attack paths: independent layers, each catching what the previous missed, sized so that breaching one buys an attacker the *next layer*, not the crown jewels. This page is the layered model, the zero-trust reframe, the edge's special problems, and the five-minute checklist that security-reviews any design you'll ever present.

## Zero trust: the perimeter is dead, identity is the perimeter

The castle-and-moat model — hard shell, soft interior, "inside the VPN = trusted" — died of its own success stories: one phished credential or [compromised pod](../devops/containers.md) inside the shell, and the flat interior network was a buffet. **Zero trust** is the replacement posture, and it's three sentences, not a product: **every request is authenticated and authorized** regardless of network origin ([workload identity + mTLS everywhere](../devops/service-mesh.md) — the mesh made it deployable); **least privilege everywhere** (identities carry [minimum scopes](authn-authz.md), [SG-to-SG rules name exact flows](../devops/cloud-networking.md), the [egress allowlist](../networking/proxies-gateways.md) bounds what leaves); **assume breach** — design as if one component is *already* compromised and ask what it can reach ([blast radius](../foundations/reliability-availability.md), attacker edition).

Note what zero trust did *not* do: delete the network layers. [Isolated subnets](../devops/cloud-networking.md), private endpoints, and segmentation still stand — as *one more independent layer*, no longer the only one. That's the depth in defense in depth.

## The layers, walked outside-in

- **Edge** — [DDoS absorption at the CDN/anycast layer](../networking/cdn.md) (volumetric attacks die in the provider's capacity, which exceeds yours by orders of magnitude), WAF for the signature-shaped attacks, [rate limiting per key/IP with fail-closed auth endpoints](../distributed/rate-limiting.md), TLS termination with modern config. The design point interviews reward: **cheap rejection early** — [the same philosophy as load shedding](../distributed/resilience.md): every hostile request stopped at the edge never costs interior resources (and the [penetration-attack/cache-miss genre](../caching/failure-modes.md) is exactly this principle applied to fabricated keys).
- **Application** — [authn/authz](authn-authz.md) enforced at the resource; input validation at every trust boundary (the OWASP evergreen trio in one sentence: parameterized queries kill injection, output encoding kills XSS, and *never trust client-supplied identity/price/quantity* — re-derive server-side); [webhook signatures + replay windows](../networking/apis.md); [idempotency keys as fraud-dedup](../messaging/delivery-semantics.md).
- **Platform** — [signed images admitted only](../devops/containers.md), [pipeline as trust boundary](../devops/cicd.md), [secrets short-lived and attested](../devops/secrets-identity.md), [policy-as-code refusing public buckets before they exist](../devops/iac-gitops.md). The platform layer is where one engineer's control protects four hundred repos — [the paved road](../caching/failure-modes.md) is a security architecture.
- **Data** — encryption in transit (mTLS interior, TLS edge) and at rest ([envelope encryption](../devops/secrets-identity.md)); **crypto-shredding** as the deletion story; classification driving handling ([PII never in logs](../observability/logging.md), [residency by homing](../devops/multi-region.md)); and the unsung hero, **immutability**: [object-lock on backups and audit trails](../data/object-storage.md) — the ransomware defense and the compliance artifact in one control.
- **Detection & response** — the layer that assumes the others failed: [audit logs on an immutable, separate stream](../observability/logging.md), anomaly alerting on auth patterns, [flow logs enabled before the incident](../devops/cloud-networking.md), and an [incident process](../observability/incidents.md) that treats "breach" as a rehearsed scenario ([game days](../observability/chaos.md) include security tabletops). Detection converts "compromised for nine months" into "compromised for ninety minutes" — at scale, that difference *is* the security program.

## Threat modeling, right-sized

The heavyweight frameworks (STRIDE et al.) compress, for design-review purposes, into four questions asked of every arrow and box — [the four questions'](../foundations/thinking-in-systems.md) security twin: **What's the asset here?** (data, money, compute, reputation). **Who's the attacker and what's their budget?** (drive-by scanner ≠ organized fraud ≠ insider — controls scale to threat, not to paranoia). **What's the path?** (walk each trust boundary: client→edge, service→service, [pipeline→prod](../devops/cicd.md), vendor→you — the boundary *crossings* are where controls live). **What's the blast radius when this control fails?** (assume breach, bound the damage). Fifteen minutes of this per design beats a hundred pages of policy nobody reads — and *narrating* it in an interview ("the asset is the payment token; the boundary crossing that worries me is the webhook from the processor; here's the signature check and replay window") is Staff-grade signal.

## The five-minute design review checklist

Run this against any architecture — yours, a colleague's, an interview whiteboard:

1. **Identity**: every hop authenticated? [Tokens scoped and short?](authn-authz.md) Machine identities [attested, not static](../devops/secrets-identity.md)?
2. **Authorization**: enforced at the resource, deny-by-default, [staleness of cached permissions bounded](../caching/fundamentals.md)?
3. **Edge**: [rate limits](../distributed/rate-limiting.md) (fail-closed on auth), DDoS story, input validation at first touch?
4. **Secrets**: none in [git/CI/env-baked images](../devops/secrets-identity.md); rotation is [dual-validity](../devops/secrets-identity.md); [cert expiry alarmed](../devops/secrets-identity.md)?
5. **Network**: [tiered subnets, SG-to-SG, egress allowlisted](../devops/cloud-networking.md)?
6. **Data**: encrypted both states, classified, [deletable on demand](../data/object-storage.md), [absent from logs](../observability/logging.md)?
7. **Supply chain**: [images signed and admitted-by-policy, pipeline OIDC-federated](../devops/cicd.md)?
8. **Detection**: audit stream immutable and separate? Would you *know* within the hour?
9. **Blast radius**: one compromised service — what can it reach? ([The assume-breach walk.](#zero-trust-the-perimeter-is-dead-identity-is-the-perimeter))
10. **The people path**: break-glass audited? Offboarding revokes *everything*? ([The insider is a threat model too.](../devops/iac-gitops.md))

!!! ops "DevOps lens"
    Security operations is mostly *hygiene at scale*, and DevOps owns the machinery: **patching as a pipeline** ([base-image rebuilds on CVE feeds](../devops/containers.md), fleet-rolled with [canary discipline](../devops/deployments.md) — patch speed is a security SLO), **posture as code** ([policy engines](../devops/iac-gitops.md) scanning for public buckets, permissive SGs, unencrypted volumes — findings auto-filed, [drift](../devops/iac-gitops.md) treated as regression), **the scanner triad** (dependencies, images, IaC — in CI, blocking on criticals, with a [flake-budget-style](../devops/cicd.md) noise discipline so teams don't learn to ignore them), and **credential-leak response as a rehearsed runbook** (rotate-first-investigate-second; [short-lived credentials](../devops/secrets-identity.md) shrink this from crisis to non-event — the best incident is the one whose stolen token expired an hour ago). The cultural note that makes it stick: security findings routed like [error-budget burn](../observability/slos.md) — visible, owned, prioritized by the same machinery as reliability, not filed in a parallel universe nobody visits.

!!! staff "Staff+ altitude"
    Markers: (1) **Security is a property of defaults** — the Staff-level insight is that org-wide security lives in the [paved road](../caching/failure-modes.md) (the template with mTLS on, the pipeline with signing built in, the bucket module that can't go public), not in review vigilance; every control moved from "remembered" to "inherited" is permanent risk reduction. (2) **Threat-model the org, not just the app** — the [pipeline](../devops/cicd.md), the [flag system](../devops/deployments.md), the IdP, the laptop fleet: the highest-leverage attacks target the systems that control systems, and the architecture review that skips them reviewed the wrong architecture. (3) **Compliance is a by-product of good design** — SOC 2/ISO controls map almost 1:1 onto this page plus [secrets](../devops/secrets-identity.md) and [audit logging](../observability/logging.md); build the platform right and the audit is evidence-gathering, not engineering ([the same claim as secrets](../devops/secrets-identity.md), because it keeps being true). (4) **Risk acceptance is a written decision** — "we accept the 60-second permission-staleness window because the alternative costs 15 ms per request" is legitimate *when documented and owned*; silent risk acceptance is just risk, and the Staff artifact is the register where these trades live.

!!! interview "In the interview"
    Security scoring in design interviews is mostly *defaults and reflexes* — collect the points as you go: TLS assumed everywhere, [tokens scoped and short](authn-authz.md), webhooks signed, [rate limits with fail-closed auth](../distributed/rate-limiting.md), secrets [attested not stored](../devops/secrets-identity.md), buckets private by policy. Then one deliberate set piece when the design stabilizes — the **assume-breach walk**: *"if this worker is compromised: it holds a 15-minute scoped token, reaches only the queue and its own bucket path via [egress allowlist](../networking/proxies-gateways.md), can't see other tenants' keys, and its anomalous S3 listing shows in flow logs within minutes — blast radius is one tenant-hour of data."* That paragraph is defense in depth *demonstrated*, and it's more convincing than any recitation of layers. Probes: *"how do you protect against DDoS?"* (edge absorption + cheap early rejection + [the priority-shedding continuum](../distributed/resilience.md) — same machinery, hostile traffic); *"what's zero trust actually?"* (three sentences, not a vendor: authenticate every request, least privilege, assume breach — then note the network layers remain as depth); *"how do you handle a leaked credential?"* (rotate-first runbook + the structural answer: short-lived attested credentials make the question small).
