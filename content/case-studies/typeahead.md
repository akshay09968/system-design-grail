# Typeahead

Design search autocomplete: suggestions appearing under a search box within a keystroke's patience. The prompt looks small and is secretly a masterclass in [read-path optimization](../foundations/thinking-in-systems.md) — the strictest latency budget in the canon (double-digit milliseconds, *per keystroke*, at massive QPS), solved by precomputing almost everything and caching the rest. If [the feed](news-feed.md) is the write-time-vs-read-time trade at product scale, typeahead is the same trade compressed into one endpoint.

## Requirements & estimation

**Scope**: prefix → top-K suggestions (K≈10), ranked by popularity, updated as trends shift (minutes-fresh, not seconds — *ask*, because it halves the design); personalization and spell-tolerance parked as layers. Non-functional: **p99 < 50 ms server-side** (the box feels broken beyond ~100 ms end-to-end), and the QPS multiplier that defines the problem: **every keystroke is a query** — a 10-character search = 10 requests.

**Numbers**: 100M searches/day × ~8 keystrokes ≈ 8×10⁸ lookups/day ≈ **~10k QPS average, 30k+ peak** — read-QPS on par with the main search engine itself, for a *decorative* feature. Corpus: top 10M queries; storing top-10 per prefix for prefixes up to length ~8 ≈ tens of GB — **fits in RAM across a small cluster**. **Verdict**: "this is a pure read-serving problem over a smallish precomputed dataset — the design is the data structure, the caching, and the pipeline that refreshes it."

## The core structure: precomputed top-K per prefix

The naive answer (trie walked at query time, collecting and ranking descendants) dies on the budget: a popular prefix like "s" has millions of descendants — you cannot rank them per keystroke. The fix is the prompt's whole lesson, [the precompute lever](../foundations/scalability.md) at maximum: **store the answer, not the data** — for every prefix, the top-K suggestions, *already ranked*:

- **Structure**: a hash map `prefix → [top-K]` is honestly sufficient (and easier to shard than a trie — say this; the trie-vs-map pragmatism reads as experience, not ignorance). A trie with top-K cached *at each node* is the classic answer and fine too; the map wins on operational simplicity, [partitioning](../data/partitioning.md) (hash the prefix), and rebuild simplicity.
- **Serving**: request "sys" → one hash lookup → return 10 strings. [Memory-speed](../foundations/estimation.md), O(1), no ranking at read time — the 50 ms budget closes with ~45 ms to spare, which is exactly the headroom the [network path](../networking/fundamentals.md) needs.
- **Sharding**: by prefix hash; [hot prefixes](../caching/failure-modes.md) (every user types "t") get the [L1-cache-on-serving-nodes treatment](../caching/failure-modes.md) — short-prefix results are so hot and so small that every edge node can hold them.

**Edge caching goes further here than anywhere**: responses are tiny, shared across all users (pre-personalization), and keyed by prefix — [CDN-cacheable with 60 s TTLs](../networking/cdn.md) at hit rates that beggar belief, because prefix popularity is [Zipf on steroids](../caching/fundamentals.md). A double-digit percentage of the fleet's QPS can terminate at the edge. (Client-side debounce — fire after ~100 ms of typing pause — plus client caching of just-typed prefixes cuts another large slice; *the client is part of the read path*, [as ever](../foundations/consistency-models.md).)

## The refresh pipeline: where writes went

Nothing updates at query time; popularity flows through a [batch/streaming pipeline](../data/analytics.md) — the [CQRS shape](../messaging/event-driven.md), unusually pure (the serving store is *entirely* a projection):

> Search logs → [stream aggregation](../messaging/kafka.md) (query counts, sliding windows with decay — yesterday's hit shouldn't outrank this hour's trend; exponential decay is the one-line answer) → periodic top-K computation per prefix → **build the new serving snapshot** → atomic swap.

The swap is the operational elegance worth narrating: build the next generation of the prefix map *offline*, verify it (sanity checks: size deltas, [canary queries](../devops/deployments.md), the blocklist below), then flip serving to the new snapshot — [versioned, instantly rollbackable](../devops/deployments.md), no in-place mutation of the hot structure ever ([immutable-snapshot serving](../data/object-storage.md) — the same pattern as search-index deploys and [feed-model rollouts](news-feed.md); naming the family is the flourish). Trend freshness = pipeline cadence (5–15 min batch, or a small streaming lane layered on top for breakout queries — the hybrid, if the interviewer pushes freshness).

**The filter nobody remembers until production**: suggestions are user-generated content that your product *actively recommends* — profanity, harassment targets, legal removals, and spam campaigns (bots typing a phrase to make it "trend") all need a **blocklist + anomaly detection in the pipeline**, applied at build time (cheap, auditable) with an emergency path that can purge a suggestion from serving in minutes ([the kill switch](../devops/deployments.md), again). Raising this unprompted is a strong product-maturity signal.

**Personalization, scoped honestly**: full per-user ranking re-breaks the caching that makes the design work. The pragmatic layer: global top-K from the shared structure + a client-side/user-service re-rank over that small candidate set (your recent searches float up) — [candidate generation vs. ranking, the feed's lesson](news-feed.md), recursed. Say the trade: personalization depth costs cache hit rate, and the budget picks the winner.

!!! ops "DevOps lens"
    The operational story is snapshot-shaped: **build-pipeline health is the freshness SLO** ([staleness alert on snapshot age](../observability/alerting.md) — a wedged build means suggestions quietly fossilize; the dead-man's-switch pattern applies), **swap safety** (each snapshot [canaried against golden queries](../devops/deployments.md) — "empty results for 'a'" must fail the build, not reach users; rollback = re-point at the previous snapshot, seconds), **serving-tier flatness** (per-node p99 and [hot-prefix skew](../caching/failure-modes.md) — the L1 layer should flatten it; if one shard runs hot, a short prefix leaked past the edge cache), and **QPS hygiene at the front door** ([per-client rate limits](rate-limiter.md) because scrapers *love* typeahead endpoints — they're a free query-popularity API if you don't meter them, which is also a competitive-intelligence leak worth one security sentence).

!!! staff "Staff+ altitude"
    (1) **Typeahead is a product surface with editorial liability** — what you suggest is what you *endorse*: the blocklist governance (who decides, how fast, audit trail) is a policy system wearing an infra costume, and the Staff move is designing the *process* alongside the pipeline. (2) **The snapshot pattern is a platform asset** — build-verify-swap-rollback over immutable artifacts generalizes to search indexes, ML feature stores, and [config planes](feature-flags.md); building it once as shared machinery is the [paved-road](../caching/failure-modes.md) play. (3) **Freshness tiers by query class** — head queries (stable, cache-forever) vs. trending tail (streaming lane) lets you buy breakout-freshness *without* re-architecting the whole pipeline — [the asymmetric-workload split](news-feed.md), third appearance. (4) **Measure the product, not just the service**: suggestion acceptance rate (did the user click one?) is the metric that justifies the entire system's existence — and connects the infra conversation to the business one, which is the altitude shift in miniature.

!!! interview "In the interview"
    The spine: keystroke-QPS math → "precompute top-K per prefix, hash map over trie for ops simplicity" → the edge/client caching stack → the refresh pipeline with atomic snapshot swap → blocklist + rate limits as the maturity close. Probes to expect: *why not compute at query time?* (millions of descendants under short prefixes — the budget dies; precompute moves the work to write time [where all read-optimization lives](../foundations/thinking-in-systems.md)); *how fresh are trends?* (pipeline cadence + the streaming lane for breakouts — and *ask what the product needs* before gold-plating); *how do you handle typos?* (park it honestly: fuzzy matching is a different structure — edit-distance candidates or n-grams — layered behind the exact path so the hot path stays O(1)); *what breaks at 10×?* (nothing interesting — the dataset barely grows; QPS scales with [stateless serving + more edge cache](../foundations/scalability.md); the pipeline is the only tier that notices). The memorable close: "this is the purest read-path system in the canon — everything hard happens before the user types."
