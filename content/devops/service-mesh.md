# Service Mesh

Take everything the [resilience page](../distributed/resilience.md) prescribed — timeouts, retries with budgets, circuit breakers, outlier ejection — plus [mTLS everywhere](secrets-identity.md), traffic splitting, and per-hop telemetry, and ask: *how do forty teams in eleven languages get all of it, uniformly, without forty reimplementations?* The mesh's answer: put a proxy next to every workload and make the network programmable. It's the [proxy page's](../networking/proxies-gateways.md) logic taken to its conclusion — and like every powerful centralization, it trades local complexity for a new, concentrated kind.

## The architecture: sidecars and a control plane

A small L7 proxy (Envoy, in practice) deploys beside each workload instance ([the pod's shared network namespace](kubernetes-workloads.md) makes this trivial); traffic is transparently redirected through it, so every service-to-service call traverses two proxies — client's sidecar out, server's sidecar in. The **control plane** (Istio, Linkerd's) holds intent — routing rules, security policy, telemetry config — and pushes it to the fleet of proxies (the **data plane**) as [versioned, watched configuration](kubernetes-architecture.md).

The reusable design shape (you've seen it [before](kubernetes-architecture.md)): brains centralized, muscle distributed, and — done right — **fail static**: proxies keep operating on their last-known config when the control plane is down; you lose *changes*, not *traffic*. Any mesh (or mesh-like system) that fails closed on control-plane loss has repeated the [blast-radius mistake](../distributed/consensus.md) the pattern exists to avoid — it's the first question to ask any vendor.

## What it buys (the three pillars)

**Zero-trust security, structurally.** Every sidecar holds a [workload identity certificate](secrets-identity.md) (SPIFFE-style, auto-rotated); every hop is **mTLS** — encrypted, mutually authenticated, with authorization policy ("payments may call ledger; nothing else may") enforced at the receiving sidecar. This converts "encrypt internal traffic and prove who's calling" from a 40-team migration into platform config — for most adopters, *the* business case, because [compliance regimes](../security/index.md) increasingly demand exactly this.

**Resilience as configuration.** [Timeouts, retries-with-budgets, circuit breaking, outlier ejection](../distributed/resilience.md) — declared once, enforced fleet-wide, in any language: the [paved road](../caching/failure-modes.md) argument at its strongest, because resilience folklore is exactly the kind of knowledge that otherwise exists in three teams and zero others. Plus [proper L7 load balancing](../networking/load-balancing.md) for gRPC (the pins-to-one-pod fix), locality-aware routing ([the cross-AZ bill shrinker](cloud-networking.md)), and **traffic splitting by percentage/header** — the primitive under [canary deploys](deployments.md).

**Observability without instrumentation.** Every hop emits uniform [RED metrics](../observability/metrics.md), access logs, and [trace spans](../observability/tracing.md) (with the honest caveat: proxies propagate trace *context* only if apps forward the headers — the mesh sees hops, not causality, without app cooperation). The uniform service-to-service dashboard, for free, in every language including the legacy one nobody instruments.

## What it costs (the honest bill)

- **Latency & compute**: two proxy traversals per hop — ~0.5–2 ms and real CPU/memory per pod, multiplied by every pod. On [latency budgets](../foundations/latency-throughput.md) already tight, the mesh is a line item; the [middlebox-tax audit](../networking/proxies-gateways.md) applies.
- **Config blast radius**: one bad routing rule or authz policy, pushed fleet-wide, *is* the outage ([the config-push genre's](../networking/proxies-gateways.md) largest stage). Mesh config is production code: canaried, versioned, staged.
- **Operational depth**: you now run a distributed system that intercepts all traffic — upgrade choreography (sidecar version skew), certificate infrastructure, control-plane capacity, and a new debugging layer ("is it the app, the sidecar, or the policy?" — every network mystery gains a suspect).
- **The trend to know**: **sidecar-less meshes** (Istio ambient, Cilium's [eBPF](linux-fundamentals.md) approach) move the interception into per-node proxies/kernel — cheaper per-pod, coarser isolation; the design space is actively moving, which is itself worth saying in interviews.

## When it's worth it

The adoption math is about *N and heterogeneity*: **many services × many languages × compliance pressure** → the mesh consolidates what you'd otherwise build badly N times (mTLS alone often justifies it). **Few services, one language, one team** → a good shared library ([resilience4j-class](../distributed/resilience.md) + your RPC client) delivers 80% of the value with none of the platform tax — and saying *that* is the credibility move: the mesh is an organizational technology, and organizations below its complexity threshold should decline it by name.

!!! ops "DevOps lens"
    Mesh operations concentrate in four places: **upgrades** (control plane, then sidecars — which means restarting every pod in the fleet; version-skew windows are where the weird bugs live; automate with rollout waves and [soak time](deployments.md)), **certificates** (auto-rotation is the feature *and* the failure mode — a broken CA or clock-skewed node manifests as fleet-wide connection refusals with [the cert-expiry incident's](../networking/dns.md) signature; monitor cert ages and rotation success as tier-0), **config safety** (validate, canary to one namespace, watch golden signals, then widen — the same ceremony as any [fleet config push](../networking/proxies-gateways.md)), and **the debugging layer** (teach the org to read sidecar status codes and policy-denial logs, or every app team's first mesh year is spent blaming Envoy — usually correctly enough to be annoying). And keep the escape hatch documented: per-workload mesh exclusion for the bleeding-edge protocol or the latency-critical path that genuinely can't pay the toll.

!!! staff "Staff+ altitude"
    Markers: (1) **Adopt for the org you have** — the mesh-vs-library decision is a written cost-benefit with N, language count, and compliance obligations as inputs; adopting Istio at 8 services in one language is résumé-driven platform engineering, and declining it *with the criteria documented* is the stronger signal. (2) **The mesh is a policy engine — govern it like one**: authz policies, retry budgets, and traffic rules need ownership, review paths, and drift detection ([GitOps](iac-gitops.md) for mesh config is not optional at scale). (3) **Budget the tax explicitly** — mesh CPU/latency belongs in [capacity plans](cost-capacity.md) and latency budgets as a named line, revisited as sidecar-less options mature. (4) **Fail-static is your acceptance test** — before adoption, kill the control plane in staging and verify traffic flows; the answer defines whether you bought resilience infrastructure or a new [single point of correlation](../foundations/reliability-availability.md).

!!! interview "In the interview"
    Deploy the mesh *by its pillars, with its bill*: *"Service-to-service concerns live in the mesh — mTLS with workload identity, retry budgets and circuit breaking as config, canary traffic splits, uniform RED metrics — costing a millisecond or two per hop and a config plane we canary like code; at our service count that beats N library ports."* One sentence of features, one of costs, one of justification — that shape *is* the senior answer. Probes: *"what does the sidecar actually do?"* (transparent L7 proxy: policy, TLS, LB, telemetry — [Envoy's job description](../networking/proxies-gateways.md)); *"what happens when the control plane dies?"* (fail static — last-known config serves; changes freeze; and you *tested* this); *"mesh vs. API gateway?"* ([east-west vs. north-south](../networking/proxies-gateways.md) — consumers-and-contracts at the edge, service-identity inside); *"would you adopt it?"* — the N-and-heterogeneity math, out loud, with the library alternative named. Bonus depth: mention ambient/eBPF sidecar-less as the direction of travel, one sentence, no more.
